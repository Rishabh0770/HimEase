Discover Kangra

Welcome to the mesmerizing Kangra District, a land where spirituality, adventure, and natural beauty blend seamlessly. Begin your journey with the holy town of Dharamshala and the Tibetan charm of McLeodganj. Day two takes you to the lush Palampur Tea Gardens surrounded by the mighty Dhauladhar ranges. On day three, visit the historic Kangra Fort, one of the oldest forts in India. Next, experience the thrill of paragliding at Bir Billing, Asia’s top paragliding site. Your adventure in Kangra is a perfect mix of spirituality, history, and adrenaline.

Nestled in the lap of the Dhauladhar Mountains, Kangra is blessed with tea gardens, temples, and snow-clad peaks. Its towns like Dharamshala and Palampur are known for Tibetan culture, monasteries, and breathtaking views. The Kangra Fort narrates stories of history, while Bir Billing invites adventure seekers for unforgettable flights.

Kangra is an all-round destination offering spiritual getaways, cultural explorations, and adventure activities. From peaceful monasteries to thrilling paragliding flights, every corner of Kangra has something special for travelers.

The Enchanting Beauty of Kangra

  • Kangra Fort: One of the largest forts in the Himalayas, offering a glimpse into the region’s royal history and panoramic valley views.
  • Masroor Rock Cut Temples: Ancient monolithic temples carved from rock, often called the “Ellora of the Himalayas.”
  • Baijnath Temple: A 13th-century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its exquisite Nagara-style architecture.
  • Mcleodganj & Dharamshala: Famous for Tibetan culture, monasteries, the Dalai Lama’s residence, and vibrant cafés.
  • Kangra Valley: Surrounded by lush tea gardens, pine forests, and the backdrop of the Dhauladhar range, perfect for nature lovers.

Experiences in Kangra

  • Trekking: Popular treks include Triund, Kareri Lake, and Indrahar Pass, offering breathtaking Himalayan views.
  • Spiritual Exploration: Visit monasteries in Dharamshala and ancient temples spread across the valley for a cultural-spiritual blend.
  • Tea Gardens: Walk through the scenic Kangra tea estates and taste authentic Kangra green tea.
  • Paragliding: Head to nearby Bir-Billing, one of the world’s top paragliding sites, for an adrenaline-filled adventure.

How to Reach Kangra

Kangra is well connected by road to Delhi, Chandigarh, and other North Indian cities. The nearest airport is Kangra Airport (Gaggal), about 15 km from Dharamshala. The nearest railway station is Pathankot, with a narrow-gauge line to Kangra. Regular buses and taxis operate from Dharamshala, Palampur, and Chandigarh.

FAQ'S

What is the best time to visit Kangra? ▼
The best time to visit Kangra is from March to June and September to November. Summers are pleasant, while autumn offers clear skies for paragliding.
Which are the must-visit attractions in Kangra? ▼
Top attractions include Dharamshala, McLeodganj, Palampur Tea Gardens, Kangra Fort, and Bir Billing.
Is Kangra suitable for adventure activities? ▼
Yes! Kangra offers paragliding at Bir Billing, trekking, camping, and nature walks in tea estates.
How can I reach Kangra? ▼
Kangra is well-connected by road from Delhi and Chandigarh. The nearest airport is Gaggal Airport (Dharamshala), and the nearest railway station is Pathankot.
What food is Kangra famous for? ▼
Kangra is famous for Himachali Dham, local siddu, thukpa, momos, and freshly brewed Kangra tea.
